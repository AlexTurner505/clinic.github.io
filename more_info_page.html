Endoscopy Treatment

Endoscopy is a minimally invasive procedure used to examine the interior of the digestive tract. It involves the use of a thin, flexible tube with a camera and light attached to it (endoscope). This instrument is inserted through the mouth or rectum to visualize and diagnose various conditions such as ulcers, inflammation, tumors, or bleeding in the gastrointestinal tract.

Image Source: Example.com

During the procedure, the patient may receive sedation to ensure comfort. The endoscope transmits live images to a monitor, allowing the doctor to examine the inner lining of the digestive system and, if needed, perform certain treatments or collect tissue samples for biopsy.

Endoscopy is a valuable tool for diagnosing digestive issues and guiding further treatment plans. It's generally a safe procedure with minimal risks and a quick recovery time.
